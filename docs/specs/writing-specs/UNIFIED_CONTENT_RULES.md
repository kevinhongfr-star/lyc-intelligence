# PART I — VOICE PRINCIPLES

## 1. The Double Layer Principle

Everything operates on two layers simultaneously:
- **Layer 1 (Surface):** Data points. Observable situations. Structural descriptions. Market facts. Nothing emotional. Nothing evaluative.
- **Layer 2 (Depth):** Recognition. Urgency. Anxiety. "This is happening to me." This layer is **NEVER stated explicitly**. It is triggered by the precision and specificity of Layer 1.

**The Rule:** If you write "this is concerning" or "leaders are worried" — you have failed. The fact itself must create the concern. If a CEO reads "CEO tenure has declined from 10 years to 5.5 years" and doesn't feel something — fix it by making the fact more specific, not by adding emotion.

---

## 2. Voice Principles

| Principle | Meaning | Example | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Expert** | We know our stuff but don't lecture | "Your shortlist shows 3 candidates above 85% alignment" | "Based on multivariate analysis of candidate parameters..." |
| **Direct** | Clear without being cold | "This candidate withdrew" | "Regrettably, the candidate in question has elected to discontinue..." |
| **Confident** | We trust our system — not arrogant, just clear | "The match score reflects strong alignment" | "Our AI is the best in the industry" |
| **Human** | Professional warmth | "Congratulations on the successful placement" | "Woo you nailed it!!" |

# PART II — BANNED PATTERNS

## 3. What We Never Say

Absolute rules. No exceptions across any asset type.

| Never say | Why | What to do instead |
|---|---|---|
| "We are luxury/premium/boutique/exclusive" | Positioning must be implied, never declared | Let content quality, specificity, and audience caliber signal this |
| "We are different from [competitor]" | Never mention or compare to competitors | Describe what we do with enough specificity that the reader draws their own comparisons |
| "We diagnose" | Implies clinical detachment | Use "assess," "map," "identify," "surface," "make visible" |
| "Leaders feel scared/anxious/overwhelmed" | Telling the reader what they feel is condescending | Describe the structural situation with precision that triggers the feeling |
| "The future is uncertain/unpredictable" | Vague. Every consultancy says this | Name the specific structural shift. Quantify it |
| "Unprecedented times/challenges" | Cliché. Zero impact | State the data. Let the reader decide |
| "Game-changer/disruptive/revolutionary" | Marketing language. Undermines credibility | State what is changing, how fast, with what measurable effect |
| "We help leaders navigate complexity" | Every firm says this. Zero differentiation | Name the specific friction, force, and resolution |
| "Best-in-class/world-class" | Empty superlatives | Show, do not tell |
| "In today's rapidly changing environment" | Filler phrase. Means nothing | Start with the specific fact |
| "Thought leadership" | Self-declared. Reader decides | Produce content that is thought leadership by its quality |
| "Holistic/integrated/end-to-end" (without specificity) | Vague claims require evidence | Name the specific components and how they connect |
| "Empower/enable/support" (as verbs for what we do) | Generic consulting language | Name the specific action and its measurable outcome |

# PART III — CONTENT PRODUCTION RULES

## 4. Specificity Rules

Every claim needs a number, a source, or an observable situation. No unsupported adjectives.

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Leaders face enormous pressure" | "CEO tenure has declined from 10 years (2000) to 5.5 years (2025)" |
| "AI is disrupting management" | "Mid-management layers built on information processing are being restructured at higher speed and lower error rates by AI systems" |
| "Skills are becoming outdated faster" | "The half-life of a learned professional skill has compressed from 10-15 years to 3-5 years" |

## 5. Structural Framing

Always frame at the organization/system level, not the individual level.

| Wrong (individual) | Right (structural) |
|---|---|
| "Leaders feel overwhelmed by the pace of change" | "The system is not designed for this tempo" |
| "Managers are anxious about AI" | "The capabilities that justified mid-management layers for three decades are now executable by AI" |
| "Professionals worry their skills are becoming irrelevant" | "Domain expertise accumulated over 15-20 years is being revalued" |

## 6. The Three Forces — Approved Language

Use exact language. Do not paraphrase.

| Force | Approved Name | Approved Description | Never Say |
|---|---|---|---|
| Force 1 | **China's operating model going global** | China's influence has shifted from market presence to operating model export — the underlying logic of how Chinese organizations build strategy, make decisions, manage talent, and scale operations is becoming a competitive reference point that affects every company regardless of whether they operate in China. | "Chinese competition is intensifying" / "China threat" / "Asia rising" |
| Force 2 | **AI dissolving established foundations** | The measurable impact of AI on leadership is not job displacement — it is the systematic erosion of the capabilities that constituted leadership value for the past three decades. | "AI is taking over" / "automation apocalypse" / "digital disruption" |
| Force 3 | **The generational speed gap** | The tempo at which markets, technologies, and organizational dynamics shift has decoupled from the tempo at which leadership development systems operate. | "Everything is changing so fast" / "the pace of disruption" / "accelerating change" |

## 7. Tone by Asset Type

| Asset Type | Tone |
|---|---|
| **Newsletter** | Analytical observation. One friction, explored deeply. |
| **Webinar deck** | Presentation of evidence. Building a case. |
| **Workshop** | Interactive, question-driven. Participants discover their own friction. |
| **Diagnostic report** | Clinical precision. No judgment. Just what the data shows. |
| **Advisory proposal** | Professional, specific, scoped. No marketing language. |
| **LinkedIn post** | One observation. One data point. One question. |
| **Email/DM** | One specific thing. No pitch. Maximum 80 words. |
| **Podcast** | Conversational but substantive. |
| **Web page** | Clear, scannable, specific. |

## 8. Progressive Disclosure

Never pitch the full stack. Every communication addresses ONE specific thing.

| Context | Rule |
|---|---|
| LinkedIn post | One force. One data point. One question. No CTA to the full Forum. |
| Newsletter issue | One friction, explored deeply. CTA to one specific next step. |
| DM to prospect | One observation. No pitch. Offer to share one relevant piece of content. |
| Webinar | One force or intersection. CTA to diagnostic. |
| Workshop | Hands-on exploration. CTA to diagnostic + advisory conversation. |
| Diagnostic debrief | Findings + one advisory product for the top friction. |
| Advisory proposal | Specific scope. Specific deliverables. No mention of the full stack. |

# PART IV — UX MICROCOPY LAYER

## 9. Interface State Copy (Product UI)

These rules govern **product interface feedback** — success toasts, error messages, empty states, loading indicators. Separate dimension from content production. Does not violate the Double Layer Principle (UI feedback is functional, not editorial).

| State | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Success | Warm + professional | "Mandate created. Your search is live." |
| Error | Calm + reassuring | "Something went wrong. We're on it. Try again in a moment." |
| Empty | Encouraging + clear | "No mandates yet. Let's set up your first search." |
| Loading | Transparent | "Loading your dashboard... this should take a few seconds." |
| Confirmation | Clear + final | "Candidate added to shortlist. Moving to the next step." |
| Warning | Honest + actionable | "This mandate has been open 45 days. Consider refreshing the search sweep." |
| Data-heavy | Scannable | Numbers, percentages, comparisons — not paragraphs |

### UX Copy Rules
1. No jargon without explanation — if methodology-specific, show once with tooltip
2. Numbers over adjectives — "3 of 5" not "most"
3. Active voice — "The system matched 12 candidates"
4. Front-load important — "Search completed: 47 candidates found"
5. One idea per sentence — no run-ons
6. Localize date/time to user's timezone always
7. Banned words from §3 still apply — even in UI copy

# PART V — TAXONOMY DISCLOSURE RULES

## 10. Progressive Disclosure + Taxonomy Exception

**General rule:** Progressive disclosure (§8) applies universally. One concept per communication. Never pitch the full stack.

**Exception — Concept Explanation Pages:**
When a page or asset's **primary objective is the description and explanation of a specific taxonomy concept** (e.g., a page dedicated to explaining Force 1, or a diagnostic instrument like BRIDGE, or a framework like SHIFT), that concept may be mentioned **up to 3 times** on that page.

**Conditions:**
1. The page's purpose must genuinely be the description/explanation of that concept (not a general overview page that happens to mention it)
2. Maximum 3 mentions of the taxonomy concept name per page
3. All other rules still apply: Double Layer Principle, banned words, specificity, structural framing
4. Progressive disclosure still applies to OTHER taxonomy concepts — don't mention Force 2 or Force 3 on a page dedicated to Force 1

# PART VI — APPROVED DATA POINTS

## 11. Verified Statistics (Approved for Use)

### Force 1: China's Operating Model Going Global
- Chinese companies file more patents annually than any other nation
- Innovation cycles in EVs, fintech, biotech: 6-12 months vs. 18-36 months at Western competitors
- Typical Chinese tech company makes strategic pivots in weeks; typical MNC takes quarters

### Force 2: AI Dissolving Established Foundations
- Mid-management layers built on information processing are being restructured
- Skills that justified mid-management layers are executable by AI at higher speed and lower error rates
- Domain expertise accumulated over 15-20 years is being revalued

### Force 3: The Generational Speed Gap
- Fortune 500 CEO tenure: 10 years (2000) → 5.5 years (2025); below 4 years in tech
- Half-life of learned professional skill: 10-15 years → 3-5 years
- Organizational restructuring cycles: 5-7 years → 18-24 months
- Window between market signal and required strategic response: quarters → weeks

## 12. Pre-Publication Verification (All Content)

- [ ] **Double layer test:** Facts alone trigger feeling? Remove all adjectives — does it still land?
- [ ] **Never-say test:** No prohibited phrases from §3?

- [ ] **Progressive disclosure test:** Not pitching the full stack?
