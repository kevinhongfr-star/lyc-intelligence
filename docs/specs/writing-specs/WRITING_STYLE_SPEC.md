# Executive Forum by LYC Partners — Writing Style Specification

**Version:** 1.0 |**Date:** 2026-07-12**Status:** MANDATORY for all content production. Every asset — newsletter, webinar deck, workshop, LinkedIn post, email, diagnostic report, proposal — must comply with this spec.

---

# 1. THE DOUBLE LAYER PRINCIPLE

Everything the Executive Forum produces operates on two layers simultaneously. The outer layer is factual. The inner layer is emotional. The reader should never be told what to feel — the facts themselves should trigger the feeling.

## Layer 1 — Surface (what the reader sees)

Data points. Observable business situations. Structural descriptions. Market facts. Organizational dynamics stated as observable phenomena. Nothing emotional. Nothing evaluative. Just what is happening.

## Layer 2 — Depth (what the reader feels)

Anxiety. Recognition. Urgency. The sense that "this is happening to me and I don't have a framework for it." This layer is NEVER stated explicitly. It is triggered by the precision and specificity of Layer 1.

## The Rule

If you write "this is concerning" or "leaders are worried" or "the stakes have never been higher" — you have failed. The fact itself must create the concern. If a CEO reads "CEO tenure has declined from 10 years to 5.5 years" and doesn't feel something — that is a content problem, not a tone problem. Do not fix it by adding emotion. Fix it by making the fact more specific, more concrete, more undeniable.

## Examples — Wrong vs. Right

### Example 1: Pace of Change

**WRONG** (emotional, tells the reader what to feel):
> "Leaders today face unprecedented challenges. The pace of change is overwhelming. Many executives feel the future slipping away from them. The landscape has never been more uncertain."

**RIGHT** (factual, triggers feeling through specificity):
> "The average Fortune 500 CEO tenure has declined from 10 years (2000) to 5.5 years (2025). In technology sectors, it is below 4 years. The half-life of a learned professional skill has compressed from 10-15 years to an estimated 3-5 years. Organizational restructuring cycles have shortened from 5-7 years to 18-24 months."

### Example 2: AI Impact

**WRONG:**
> "AI is dramatically disrupting how companies operate. Leaders who fail to adapt risk becoming irrelevant. The threat is real and immediate."

**RIGHT:**
> "Mid-management layers built on information processing, coordination, and reporting are being restructured. Not eliminated — restructured. The skills that justified these layers — data synthesis, status reporting, cross-functional coordination — are now executable by AI systems at higher speed and lower error rates. Domain expertise accumulated over 15-20 years is being revalued. When AI can surface patterns across datasets that no single expert could process, the premium shifts from 'knowing the answer' to 'asking the right question.'"

### Example 3: Cross-Border Pressure

**WRONG:**
> "Cross-border partnerships are under enormous pressure from geopolitical tensions. Companies must act now to protect their relationships."

**RIGHT:**
> "Every cross-border relationship involving Chinese entities now carries geopolitical interpretation risk. What was a commercial decision gets read as a political signal. What was a standard partnership gets scrutinized through a national security lens in multiple jurisdictions simultaneously. Partnerships built on contractual frameworks and quarterly KPI reviews have no structural resilience when external conditions shift."

### Example 4: Career Disruption

**WRONG:**
> "The career world is being turned upside down. Professionals must reinvent themselves or risk being left behind."

**RIGHT:**
> "The number of senior roles available has not grown proportionally to the number of professionals qualified for them. Capabilities that constituted a career advantage five years ago are now commoditized by AI or rendered irrelevant by market restructuring. Leaders who have not updated their capability architecture since their last promotion are discovering a gap between their self-assessed readiness and actual market requirements."

# 8. KEY DATA POINTS (APPROVED FOR USE)

These are the approved factual data points for use across all content. Each has been verified.

## Force 1: China's Operating Model Going Global

- Chinese companies file more patents annually than any other nation
- Innovation cycles in EVs, fintech, biotech: 6-12 months vs. 18-36 months at Western competitors
- Typical Chinese tech company makes strategic pivots in weeks; typical MNC takes quarters
- China's talent market produces leaders who manage at scale, under ambiguity, with organizational models Western frameworks cannot easily categorize
- Supply chain, manufacturing, and service models designed in China are being replicated in Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa, Latin America

## Force 2: AI Dissolving Established Foundations

- Mid-management layers built on information processing, coordination, and reporting are being restructured
- Skills that justified mid-management layers (data synthesis, status reporting, cross-functional coordination) are executable by AI at higher speed and lower error rates
- Domain expertise accumulated over 15-20 years is being revalued
- AI surfaces decision-making biases that human management layers have normalized
- Team design built on hierarchical information flow is structurally incompatible with AI-augmented workflows
- Leaders most effective in AI environments are not the ones with strongest technical backgrounds

## Force 3: The Generational Speed Gap

- Average Fortune 500 CEO tenure: 10 years (2000) to 5.5 years (2025); below 4 years in technology sectors
- Half-life of learned professional skill: compressed from 10-15 years to 3-5 years
- Organizational restructuring cycles: shortened from 5-7 years to 18-24 months
- Window between market signal and required strategic response: compressed from quarters to weeks
- Leadership development operates on a different timeline: coaching engagements run 6-12 months, programmes designed around annual cohorts, assessments updated every 2-3 years

# 9. QUALITY CHECKLIST (FOR ALL CONTENT BEFORE PUBLICATION)

Before any asset goes live, verify:

- [ ] **Double layer test:** Does this make the reader feel something through facts alone? If you removed all adjectives and adverbs, would the piece still land?
- [ ] **Never-say test:** Does this contain any of the prohibited phrases from Section 2?
- [ ] **Specificity test:** Is every claim supported by a number, a source, or an observable situation?
- [ ] **Progressive disclosure test:** Does this pitch the full stack? (It should not.)
- [ ] **Reader recognition test:** Would the target reader read this and think "this describes my situation"?
- [ ] **Conciseness test:** Can any sentence be removed without losing meaning? If yes, remove it.
- [ ] **Active voice test:** Are sentences in active voice? Is language precise?
- [ ] **CTA test:** Does this end with a specific next step?
- [ ] **Force language test:** If referencing the three forces, does it use the approved language from Section 6?
